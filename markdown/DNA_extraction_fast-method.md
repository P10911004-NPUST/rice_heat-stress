# DNA extraction

## Preparation:
| Material                       | Quantity per sample | Note                                                |
| :---:                          | :---:               | :---:                                               |
| Extraction buffer <sup>*</sup> | 400 &micro;L        |                                                     |
| Isopropanol                    | 300 &micro;L        | Around 70% of the supernatant taken from the buffer |
| Ethanol (EtOH)                 | 1000 &micro;L       | Use DNase-free ethanol and ddH<sub>2</sub>O         |
| Tris-EDTA                      | 100 &micro;L        |                                                     |


### * Extraction buffer
> 200 mM Tris-HCl (pH 7.5)  
> 250 mM NaCl  
> 25 mM EDTA  
> 0.5% SDS  

| Stock solution          | Addition |
| :---:                   | :---:    |
| 1 M Tris-HCl (pH 7.5)   | 20 mL    |
| 5 M NaCl                | 5 mL     |
| 500 mM EDTA             | 5 mL     |
| 10% SDS                 | 5 mL     |
| ddH<sub>2</sub>O        | 65 mL    |
|||
| Total:                  | 100 mL   |


## Procedure:
1. Cut 5-10 pieces of root tissues, powderized them after frozen with liquid nitrogen;
> Caution: if directly powderized within ependorf, after fill in liquid nitrogen into the tube, 
DO NOT close the lid, the eppendorf will exploded.
2. Add 400 &micro;L of the extraction buffer, gently inverting the tube to mix the buffer;
3. Slightly centrifuge to bring down all the mixtures to the bottom of the eppendorf; 
If many samples, they should be placed on ice;
4. Place on benchtop at room temperature for 1 hour;
5. Centrifuge at 15000 rpm for 15 minutes at room temperature to bring down the tissue residues;
6. Transfer the supernatant (~ 300&micro;L) to a new tube
7. Add 210 &micro;L isopropanol, the volume of isopropanol should be around 0.7 volume of the supernatant; 
Gently mix the isopropanol with the supernatant;
8. Keep the mixture at 4&deg;C for 24 hours or -20&deg;C for 2 hours to precipitate the DNA;
9. Centrifuge at 15000 rpm for 15 minutes at room temperature to bring down the DNA; 
Pay attention to the eppendorf position, the DNA palette should be skewed on one side of the tube bottom;
10. Discard the supernatant; Carefully absorb the supernatant by not touching or too close to the DNA palette;
11. Rinse with 70% EtOH 3 times (the volume same as the isopropanol); 
DO NOT invert or pipetting; the DNA palette should be always sticking to the tube bottom; 
Centrifuge at 15000 rpm for 10 minutes at 4&deg;C for each rinse, to make sure the DNA palette didn't float up;
12. Dry the DNA by putting it in dry incubator at 60&deg;C;
13. Add 100 &micro;L Tris-EDTA to dissolve the DNA, put in the dry incubator at 60&deg;C to accelerate the dissolution;
14. After dissolve, measure the DNA quantity and quality; the NanoDrop&trade; only requires 1 &micro;L of the DNA solution;
15. Store the DNA solution in -20&deg;C.


### Wavelength detection:
| Wavelength (nm) | Detected Compound |
| :---: | :--- |
| 230 | EtOH, EDTA, carbohydrates, phenol, Guanidone HCL (for DNA isolation) |
| 260 | RNA,ssDNA, dsDNA, guanidine isothiocyabate (for RNA isolation) |
| 270 | Phenolic solution (TRIzol for RNA isolation) |
| 280 | Protein, phenol, other contamination |

### Ideal wavelength ratio (quality indicator):
| Nucleic acid | 260 / 280 | 260 / 230 | 260 / 270 |
| :---:        | :---:     | :---:     | :---:     |
| DNA          | ~ 1.8     | > 1.8     | > 1.2     |
| RNA          | ~ 2.0     | > 1.8     | > 1.2     |
